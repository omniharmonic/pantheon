## LinkedIn Post

I built an interactive 3D map of the cultural evolution of world religion.

Pantheon visualizes 115 religious traditions, 209 shared figures, and 158 historical connections as a navigable constellation in three-dimensional space.

You can fly through the graph with keyboard controls, click on any tradition to explore its key figures and concepts, then trace cross-cultural connections — following chains like Inanna → Ishtar → Astarte → Aphrodite, or seeing how Moses appears across Judaism, Christianity, and Islam with different roles in each.

The data spans from Aboriginal Australian Dreamtime (~65,000 BCE) to modern movements like Falun Gong (1992), covering indigenous cosmologies from every continent, ancient mystery religions, syncretic diaspora traditions, and everything in between.

What surprised me most was how interconnected everything is. The Indo-European sky father (*Dyeus Pater) becomes Zeus in Greece, Jupiter in Rome, Dyaus Pita in Vedic India, and Tyr in Norse mythology — all from a single reconstructed proto-deity. Buddhist figures transform as they travel: Avalokiteshvara becomes Guanyin in China and Kannon in Japan. Yoruba orishas become Catholic saints in the Caribbean.

The whole thing runs in the browser — no install needed. Built with Three.js, Next.js, and a lot of comparative religion research.

Try it: https://omniharmonic.github.io/pantheon/

## Facebook Post

I made something I've been thinking about for a while — an interactive 3D map that lets you explore how the world's religions are connected to each other.

It's called Pantheon, and it visualizes 115 religious traditions and 209 shared figures (gods, prophets, heroes) floating in 3D space. You can fly around with your keyboard, click on anything to learn about it, and follow connection chains between figures across different cultures.

Some of the connections are wild:
→ The same Proto-Indo-European sky god became Zeus, Jupiter, and the Vedic Dyaus Pita
→ The Sumerian goddess Inanna evolved into Ishtar, then Astarte, then Aphrodite
→ Moses appears in Judaism, Christianity, AND Islam — with a different role in each
→ When Yoruba religion traveled to the Caribbean, Ogun became Saint Peter and Yemoja became the Virgin Mary

The dataset goes from Aboriginal Australian Dreamtime (~65,000 years old) all the way to modern movements. It covers indigenous traditions from every continent that are often left out of these kinds of maps — Navajo, Lakota, Dogon, Māori, and dozens more.

It runs entirely in your browser, no install needed. Just open the link and start exploring.

🔗 https://omniharmonic.github.io/pantheon/
